James Webb Space Telescope

Unveiling the mysteries of the early universe

Mission Overview

Active Mission

Exploring the Cosmos

The James Webb Space Telescope is the largest and most powerful space telescope ever built, designed to study every phase of cosmic history and reveal the universe's first galaxies.

Launch: December 25, 2021 Location: L2 Orbit Distance: 1.5 million km from Earth

Primary Objectives

Early Universe

Study the light from the first stars and galaxies that formed after the Big Bang.

Exoplanets

Analyze atmospheres of distant planets to search for potential signs of habitability.

Galaxy Evolution

Observe how galaxies form and evolve over billions of years of cosmic history.

Scientific Instruments

NIRCam

Near-Infrared Camera for imaging young stars and galaxies in the process of formation.

MIRI

Mid-Infrared Instrument for observing distant galaxies and newly forming stars.

NIRSpec

Near-Infrared Spectrograph for studying chemical compositions of celestial objects.
