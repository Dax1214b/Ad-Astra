Solar System Planets

Our cosmic neighborhood: planets and dwarf planets

Planets

Mercury

The smallest and innermost planet in the Solar System

Distance from Sun: 57.9 million km

Venus

Earth's "sister planet" with extreme greenhouse effect

Distance from Sun: 108.2 million km

Earth

Our home planet, the only known world with life

Distance from Sun: 149.6 million km

Mars

The Red Planet, target for human exploration

Distance from Sun: 227.9 million km

Jupiter

The largest planet, a gas giant with the Great Red Spot

Distance from Sun: 778.5 million km

Saturn

Known for its spectacular ring system

Distance from Sun: 1.4 billion km

Uranus

Ice giant with a unique tilted rotation axis

Distance from Sun: 2.9 billion km

Neptune

The windiest planet with supersonic storms

Distance from Sun: 4.5 billion km

Dwarf Planets

Pluto

The most famous dwarf planet, visited by New Horizons

Distance from Sun: 5.9 billion km

Ceres

The largest object in the asteroid belt

Distance from Sun: 413.7 million km

Eris

The most massive known dwarf planet

Distance from Sun: 10.1 billion km

Haumea

Known for its elongated shape and rapid rotation

Distance from Sun: 6.5 billion km

Makemake

One of the largest Kuiper Belt objects

Distance from Sun: 6.9 billion km
