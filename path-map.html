The Great North American Eclipse

The 2024 solar eclipse, also known as the Great North American Eclipse, occurred on April 8, 2024. It was a total solar eclipse, meaning the Moon completely blocked the Sun's light, creating a stunning display of the Sun's corona.

Expert Perspectives

Sir Arthur Eddington

Leading Scientist

"During the 1919 solar eclipse, Eddington led an expedition to observe the bending of starlight around the Sun, a phenomenon predicted by Einstein's theory."

Jay Pasachoff

A leading eclipse expert

"Pasachoff dedicated his career to studying solar eclipses, particularly the Sun's corona. He observed over 70 eclipses and contributed significantly to our understanding of solar and atmospheric phenomena."

Dr. Aroh Barjatya

A modern researcher

"Dr. Barjatya has focused on the effects of solar eclipses on Earth's upper atmosphere, especially the ionosphere. His leadership in NASA's Atmospheric Perturbations around Eclipse Path (APEP) mission has advanced our knowledge of how eclipses impact communication and satellite systems."
