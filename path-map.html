Path of Totality

The path of totality is the area where the total solar eclipse can be observed. This path will provide the best view of the eclipse, with the Moon completely covering the Sun for a few minutes.

To experience the total eclipse, you must be within this path. Outside of this area, only a partial eclipse will be visible.

For the best experience, plan your viewing location in advance and arrive early to secure a good spot.

Viewing Instructions

To safely view the solar eclipse, follow these essential guidelines:

  • Always use certified solar viewing glasses to protect your eyes.
  • Do not look directly at the Sun without proper eye protection.
  • Use a solar filter if viewing through a telescope or binoculars.
  • Never use regular sunglasses as they do not provide adequate protection.
  • Consider projecting the Sun's image onto a surface using a pinhole projector.
