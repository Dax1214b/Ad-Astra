Mars Perseverance Rover

Seeking signs of ancient microbial life on Mars

Mission Overview

Active Mission

Exploring Jezero Crater

The Perseverance rover is exploring an ancient river delta in Jezero Crater, searching for signs of ancient microbial life and collecting rock samples for future return to Earth.

Launch: July 30, 2020 Mars Landing: Feb 18, 2021 Location: Jezero Crater

Primary Objectives

Search for Life

Looking for signs of ancient microbial life in areas believed to be potential ancient habitable environments.

Sample Collection

Collecting rock and soil samples for future return to Earth through the Mars Sample Return campaign.

Test Technologies

Testing new technologies like the Ingenuity Mars Helicopter and MOXIE oxygen production experiment.

Scientific Instruments

SuperCam

Identifies chemical composition of rocks and soils with a camera, laser, and spectrometers.

SHERLOC

Searches for organics and minerals that have been altered by watery environments.

Mastcam-Z

Takes high-definition video, panoramic color images, and 3D images of the surface.
