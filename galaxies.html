What are Galaxies?

Galaxies are massive collections of stars, gas, dust, and dark matter held together by gravity. They come in various shapes and sizes, from spiral galaxies like our Milky Way to elliptical and irregular galaxies. Each galaxy contains billions to trillions of stars and can span hundreds of thousands of light-years across.

Types of Galaxies

Spiral Galaxies

Characterized by a central bulge surrounded by a disk of stars, gas and dust in a spiral structure.

Elliptical Galaxies

Roughly ellipsoidal shape and lack obvious structure. Generally contain older stars.

Irregular Galaxies

Neither spiral nor elliptical, often showing chaotic structure due to gravitational interactions.

Fascinating Facts

Size

The largest known galaxy, IC 1101, is over 50 times the size of our Milky Way.

Population

The observable universe contains over 100 billion galaxies.

Age

The oldest known galaxy formed about 13.4 billion years ago.
