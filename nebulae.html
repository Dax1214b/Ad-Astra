Cosmic clouds of creation and transformation

What are Nebulae?

Nebulae are vast cosmic clouds where stars are born and die. These celestial nurseries and graveyards paint the cosmos with stunning colors and shapes, telling stories of stellar evolution. From the birth of new stars to the final breaths of dying suns, nebulae showcase the dynamic nature of our universe.

Types of Nebulae

Emission Nebulae

Glowing clouds of ionized gas, energized by nearby hot stars. They appear red due to hydrogen atoms releasing energy.

Reflection Nebulae

Clouds of dust that reflect light from nearby stars, creating beautiful blue hues in space.

Planetary Nebulae

Shells of gas ejected by dying stars, creating spectacular rings and bubbles in space.

Dark Nebulae

Dense clouds of dust that block light from background stars, creating dark patches in the sky.

Fascinating Facts

Size

The largest known nebula, the Tarantula Nebula, spans 1,000 light-years across.

Temperature

Nebulae can range from extremely cold (-272°C) to incredibly hot (10,000°C).

Composition

Nebulae are made primarily of hydrogen (90%) and helium (10%), with traces of other elements.
